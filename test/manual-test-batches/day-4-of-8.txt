*Q61 [n3-24]* — DRB combination edge case: one vehicle loan plus a sundry-expense credit card facility — still WITHOUT DRB
WHO: Resident individual with exactly ONE outstanding vehicle loan and a credit card facility used solely for sundry office expenses, no other Ringgit borrowing
WHAT: Converting RM3 million from Ringgit to invest in a foreign currency fixed deposit
AMOUNT: RM 3,000,000

✅ Expected verdict: PERMITTED
📌 Expected citation: Notice 3, Para 1
🚫 Must NOT mention: RM1 million
ℹ️ Why: Notice 3 FAQ Q15 (entity DRB logic, applying the same sundry-expense exclusion via the DRB definition) and the DRB glossary: one vehicle loan plus a credit/financing facility used solely for sundry expenses does not constitute DRB — this Resident remains WITHOUT DRB under Part A, Para 1 (UNLIMITED).

*Q62 [n3-25]* — DRB combination edge case: TWO vehicle loans (no housing loan) triggers DRB
WHO: Resident individual with exactly TWO outstanding vehicle loans and no other Ringgit borrowing
WHAT: Converting RM4 million from Ringgit to invest in foreign-listed stocks
AMOUNT: RM 4,000,000

✅ Expected verdict: NOT_PERMITTED OR REQUIRES_APPROVAL
📌 Expected citation: Notice 3, Para 2
ℹ️ Why: Notice 3 FAQ Q3: having more than one (1) vehicle loan means the Resident individual is deemed to have DRB — this triggers the RM1 million/year cap (Para 2(c)), which RM4 million exceeds.

*Q63 [n3-26]* — Resident entity deemed to have DRB via its parent-subsidiary Resident Group
WHO: Resident subsidiary company with NO Ringgit borrowing of its own, but whose Resident parent (same parent-subsidiary Group) has Domestic Ringgit Borrowing
WHAT: Converting RM60 million from Ringgit this calendar year to invest in foreign currency assets

✅ Expected verdict: NOT_PERMITTED OR REQUIRES_APPROVAL

ℹ️ Why: Notice 3 FAQ Q15: a Resident entity is deemed to have DRB when another Resident entity with which it has a parent-subsidiary relationship has DRB — the RM50 million/year aggregate cap therefore applies across the Group, and RM60 million exceeds it.
